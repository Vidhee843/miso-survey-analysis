02Context — Set the Stage
3 / 8

About this analysis

The 2024 MISO Survey — cleaned, filtered, and analyzed in Python across two distinct populations.

Two populations, kept separate

Students and staff serve different roles and use different tools. n=217 students and n=146 staff after cleaning. Datasets analyzed independently to avoid conflating needs.

Data cleaned before analysis

Removed 46 incomplete student and 10 staff records. Dropped library columns, open-text fields, and metadata. Converted -99 sentinel values to null to prevent bias.

Three analytical lenses

Usage frequency, importance ratings, and satisfaction scores — compared side by side to find where expectation and reality diverge. Gap = Importance − Satisfaction.

04The Turning Point — Reframe
7 / 8

This is not a service quality crisis.
It is a service communication crisis.

The data rules out a performance explanation. High Help Desk scores, mostly positive satisfaction gaps — the services are doing their job. The problem is visibility.

Common assumption

Service quality problem

Low awareness → services must be failing → invest in new infrastructure and expanded staff.

What the data shows

Service communication problem

Services are performing well — 6.6% / 13.7% awareness with high satisfaction scores = discoverability failure, not delivery failure.

⚠

The budget risk if nothing changes

Services that appear underutilized lose funding regardless of actual quality. With only 6.6% of students and 13.7% of staff aware, utilization data will always look weak — FSU risks defunding the very services its community values most, simply because those services aren't visible enough to be used.

05Resolution — Path Forward
8 / 8

More informed users are more satisfied users.
The fix doesn't require new spending.

Students who feel "very informed" about IT score overall satisfaction 0.19 points higher than those who feel "not informed." For staff, the gap is even larger — 0.94 points across the same range.

Three targeted actions — no new infrastructure needed

1

Embed IT services in Canvas

98.6% of students are already there multiple times per week. Surface IT tools and support directly inside the platform.

2

Rebuild onboarding — separately for each group

Students and staff use completely different tools. The Canvas divide proves one message won't reach both.

3

Activate the Help Desk as communicators

Already scoring 3.72–3.88/4 across all dimensions. The most trusted touchpoint on campus — use it to promote what's available.
